**Hunting & Tailoring Update TL;DR;**

Two whole new skills, and along with it a handful of new achievements/collectibles as well as a ton of new items to hunt down\! A few things have been shuffled around, especially in GDTE to prioritize early crafting of the highly versatile Tailoring equipment.

There is a new type of activity mechanic which applies to the new skills. Normally location based activities are able to be done indefinitely as long as you have the right equipment and skill level, but most of the Hunting activities require a per completion reagent as well, making it a bit less set and forget than other activities in the game. The two Tailoring activities also make use of this mechanic, optionally taking a reagent for bonus XP.

Hunting is a new Gathering skill. Collecting animal based drops which are primarily used in Tailoring and Cooking. There are a couple of unique activity reagents like Box Traps and Kelp Drift Nets, but the primary reagents used in hunting are arrows of various tiers. The higher tier of arrow, the more Work Efficiency, and some activities require a higher tier of arrow to complete. Several new Carpentry recipes were added to craft various Hunting tools and Reagents.

Tailoring is a new Artisan skill. Tailoring is one of the first skills which allows crafting of worn equipment that can boost your stats. Tailoring crafted equipment mostly fills the Hands, Chest and Legs slots and comes in several tiers (the tiers are not necessarily strict upgrades, they have different stat priorities):

* Tatty: Global bonuses  
* Grassknot/Spikescale/Rootweave/Elderhide: All Gathering Skill bonuses  
* Apprentice/Journeyman/Spectral: All Artisan Skill bonuses  
* Shadowquill/Aerofibril: All Utility Skill bonuses

There are two new pets: a new Mummy pet which provides Tailoring bonuses and a Tiger pet which provides Hunting bonuses. Both new pets give a Global Find Collectibles boost. The shark tooth trinket can now be used to make a Hunting focused necklace with a Global \-2% steps bonus which could come into play with ultra high step count activities. There is a new Spider Trinket and associated necklace with a Global \+2 XP bonus. (The Amulet of Animal Kingdom was updated to require this new Trinket as well). Kelp Diving Gear was moved to be a Tailoring recipe.

There are several new upgradable items. 

* Camo Face Paint (Hunting Chests) \-\> Bright Camo Face Paint   
* Crafting Pants (Crafting Chests) \-\> Extra Crafty Pants  
* Durable Fabric Cutting Mat (Tailoring Chests) \-\> Golden Cutting Mat  
* Pretty Pliers (Trinketry Chests) \-\> Gem Tipped Pliers  
* Simple Sewing Needle (Various Tool Shops and Tailoring Chests) \-\> Silver/Gold Sewing Needle  
* Sturdy Fishing Rod Rest (Fishing Chests) \-\> Triple Rod Rest  
* Woodworking Glasses (Carpentry Chests) \-\> Tinted Woodworking Glasses

**Main Guide**

This guide is for those who like to take their time to fully explore and complete areas. For those who like to fill out their collection log/achievements even if it does not give any real meaningful reward. This approach will provide a slower experience than power leveling to endgame activities, but I personally find it more enjoyable.

Pros:

* Less time spent traveling and more time spent in a single zone (more AFK, less micromanaging phone)  
* Will have higher base skill level when arriving at new zones/activities  
* Faster access to the Achiever Capes (remember each collectible gives 1 Achievement point)  
* Gotta catch 'em all\!

Cons:

* Because we focus on drops instead of XP, it will be slower leveling  
* Rare drops and Collectibles come from activities, so you’ll be light on materials to craft high quality tools  
* Spends extra time on skills which you may not care about. If your aim is to be the best swordsmith, this guide will take lots of detours compared to only focusing on specific skills which work towards that goal

We'll be focusing on rare activity drops/collectibles as well as filling out our achievement log as efficiently as possible. If you want to go as quickly as possible for the Achiever capes, you can SKIP most of the collectibles and rare item drops and come back for them later after you have better gear though you will need to do a fair bit of backtracking. There are some things which you *cannot* skip (like the Soup Kitchen Badge which unlocks the Advanced Kitchen) and some which I think you *should* not skip (like the Sticky Finger Shorts and Alien Squeaky Toy which give good early game chest finding bonuses for a relatively small step investment). I've added **\!\!** as a prefix and **bolded** items which I think are critical to acquire as well as $$ as a prefix to other rare drops and \#\# to collectibles to make them easier to identify. There are a few natural places where it makes sense to pause and backtrack to gather missed collectibles: After Wine Grape Stomping when you’ll have a lot of Wine and the Treasure Hunter Outfit, after getting Jade rings, or at the very end after completing all other achievements.

**Which Attributes are best for farming rare items and collectibles?**

* Find Collectibles: Direct boost to increasing collectible drop chances. Doesn't impact rare item or chest drops, so don't get too excited about reducing the number of steps needed for the Blue Ice Sickle.  
* Work Efficiency/Steps Required: Lowers steps per action, meaning more actions, meaning more chances for that rare drop\!  
* Double Rewards/Action: A second bite at the apple  
  * The difference between these two is subtle but important. Double reward will give you a second roll on the loot table and nothing else. Double action also gives a second roll on the loot table, but also gives 2x XP and consumes 2x materials/food.   
  * Meaning Double Action is strictly better for actions that consume no resources like Traveling and Gathering. While Double Reward is generally better for resource limited activities like Crafting, Transforming or anything where you want to preserve food buffs.  
* Chest Finding: While not directly helpful for rare drops, this will get you activity chest equipment which will likely have some of the above stats  
* Fine Material Finding/Quality Outcome: Also not directly helpful, but will help with some of the trickier achievements  
* XP: One of the least useful stats for collectible hunting, but is important for unlocking activities. Especially useful for early game Trinketry where materials are severely limited.

**General Notes:**

* You can open up activity chests as you get them, they may have some juicy gear inside which will reduce your grind, but you will run into bag space issues more frequently.  
* Once you reach GDTE, you can trade in your activity chest equipment to the Mysterious Merchant for activity chips which can then be used to buy items from those chests  
* Many pieces of chest equipment can be upgraded, some are noted throughout the guide but check here for a full list before trading anything away: [https://wiki.walkscape.app/wiki/Upgraded\_Equipment](https://wiki.walkscape.app/wiki/Upgraded_Equipment)  
* Only one of the Extreme achievements has any special requirements, they just take tons of time walking. We’ll only be tracking the status of the one Extreme achievement.  
* Check the job boards every day to see if you can easily complete any jobs, advancing reputations and faction chests drops can have some great rewards.  
  * Jarvonia: Kallaheim, Azurazera  
  * Trellin: Granfiddich, Everhaven  
  * Erdwise: Blackspell Port, Everhaven  
  * Halflings: Halfling Campground  
  * Syrenthia: Vastalume  
* Remember when equipping gear to look for items with GLOBAL bonuses. For example, if you’re looking for Fine Materials on Mining equipment, you’ll miss out on things like the Rusty Spyglass and Trash Grabber. Use the in game gearset tool or the online tool [https://gear.walkscape.app](https://gear.walkscape.app) and filter by activity. This will show all gear which provides any bonus while performing that activity (even global bonuses).  
* You will need to periodically clear your bags while hunting rares. Feel free to drop or sell items you KNOW you won’t need later, but when in doubt return to a bank even if you spend some extra steps doing so.  
  * There is an achievement to hold 1 million coins, I'd highly recommend not dropping any items and banking all loot even on longer grinds  
* You can check items with requirements here in case I’ve missed any: [https://wiki.walkscape.app/wiki/Category:Items\_With\_Requirements](https://wiki.walkscape.app/wiki/Category:Items_With_Requirements)

**Low Step Count Chest/Special Loot Table Farming**

* With items like Adoring Fan Statue, Zip Pouch, Treasure Grabber, Adventuring Ring/Amulet, Amulet of Heron, Sun Stone Rings, etc you can farm for valuable drops like Coins, Activity Chests, Gems, and Adventurer’s Guild Tokens all at the same time by completing activities with low step counts. Here are some popular options.  
* **Traveling** (10 steps minimum, Agility Chests) \- Traveling short paths is the absolute best at this as you can reach a minimum of 10 steps per travel action, though you won’t be able to go completely AFK as you will need to re-travel once you reach your destination(s)  
* **Cut Birch Trees** (22 steps minimum, Woodcutting Chests) \- Lowest AFK step count activity in the game. Some people will drop the non-fine logs to stay on location longer.  
* **Mine Copper Ore** (34 steps minimum, Mining Chests) \- The best way to get Gem Pouch gems when paired with a Rock Star Amulet.  
* **Ice Sculpting** (45 steps minimum, Crafting/Carpentry Chests) \- Almost no loot to clog up your inventory. Crafting Chest gear is highly desirable for the Quality Outcome stat it provides.  
* **Guard Duty** (54 steps minimum, Agility Chests) \- Also no inventory clogging loot drops. Good source of Agility XP, one of the best sources of coins for the 1m coin achievement, best natural W.E.A.R. for Gem Pouches too. Though the higher minimum step count means special loot table rolls will be less than half than what you’d get from Cutting Birch Trees.

    * Farming Materials \- Silver or Gold Ruby Ring (If you still want to hunt for activity chest drops Gold is better. The higher the quality, the better Silver becomes for Fine Items over the Gold variant)
